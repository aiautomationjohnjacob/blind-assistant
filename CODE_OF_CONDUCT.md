# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Blind Assistant is built on the principle of "nothing about us without us." We are
committed to making this project a welcoming space for blind and visually impaired
people, disability advocates, accessibility professionals, and everyone who wants to
help build technology that genuinely changes lives.

We pledge to make participation in this community a harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of disability status, age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

### We celebrate
- Sharing lived experience with blindness and visual impairment
- Questions from people new to accessibility or assistive technology
- Constructive feedback that makes the product better for blind users
- All forms of contribution: code, feedback, testing, documentation, translation

### We do not tolerate
- Ableist language or behavior — treating disability as a tragedy, weakness, or punchline
- "Inspiration porn" — praising blind contributors for "overcoming" their blindness
- Dismissing accessibility concerns as "edge cases" or "nice to have"
- Harassment, intimidation, or unwelcome contact of any kind
- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Assuming what blind users need without listening to them

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private issue or
contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be reviewed and investigated
promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security
of the reporter.
